## Logo

**File:** `assets/logo.png` — 465×502px, RGBA PNG, transparent background.

**Description:** Hand-drawn circular brushstroke mark with "rad/s²" text centered inside. The strokes are loose, organic, ink-like — NOT geometric or mechanical. This warmth and organic quality is core to the brand identity.

**Usage rules:**
- Use on light backgrounds (the mark is dark on transparent)
- Do not apply a colored background behind it — let it sit on the page bg
- In the nav: use the logo mark (small, ~28-32px height) alongside or instead of the "RAD/S²" wordmark
- In the hero: the logo mark can coexist with the Three.js gyroscope — they complement each other (organic/brushstroke vs. precise/geometric)
- Never stretch, recolor, or add effects to the logo

## Brand Personality

Quiet confidence, operational precision, premium restraint — with organic warmth underneath.
3 words: **warm, angular, precise**

The logo being hand-drawn and the palette being off-white/sage are intentional signals: this is not a cold tech company. It's a factory built by humans, for humans.

References: Stripe product pages, Linear homepage, Apple typography, McKinsey editorial layouts.

### Color Rules
- NEVER use pure black (#000) or pure gray (#808080). Always tint warm.
- NEVER use the old burnt orange (#C86A2B). That is deprecated.
- The sage accent is used sparingly — for links, active states, key labels. Not as a background fill.
- Dark sections (hero only) use #1A1A1A as background with off-white text. The rest of the site is light.
- Avoid purple, blue-violet, or neon gradients entirely.

## Typography

Confirmed canonical from `memo.typ` in the brand docs.

| Role        | Font          | Fallback                                |
|-------------|---------------|-----------------------------------------|
| Display     | Avenir Next   | Avenir, system-ui, -apple-system, sans-serif |
| Body        | Avenir Next   | Avenir, system-ui, -apple-system, sans-serif |
| Monospace   | Menlo         | SF Mono, Consolas, monospace            |

### Typography Rules
- NEVER use Inter, IBM Plex Mono, Space Grotesk, Arial, or Helvetica as primary fonts.
- Headings: bold weight, tight letter-spacing (-0.02em), line-height ~1.1
- Body: regular weight, ~16-18px, line-height ~1.4
- Monospace labels (captions, earmarks): 7.5-8.5pt equivalent, uppercase, tracked 0.04-0.08em
- Use a modular type scale, not arbitrary sizes.

### Typographic Motif from Brand Docs
H1 headings in official brand documents use a short (~40pt) accent-colored rule beneath the heading text — a distinctive editorial mark that signals section importance. Consider adapting this to web sections: a short accent underline or rule after major headings can reinforce the brand identity.

## Layout & Spacing

- Maximum content width: ~1200-1440px
- Generous whitespace — let sections breathe. Padding between sections should feel luxurious, not cramped.
- Prefer grid-based layouts with clear visual hierarchy.
- Borders/dividers use rule-col (#D4D1CC), thin (0.5-1px), warm gray. Not currentColor or hard black.
- Cards and containers: no heavy borders. Use subtle background fills (tag-bg) or thin rule-col borders.
- No border-radius on buttons or containers — sharp edges are part of the brand identity.

## Motion & Animation

- Scroll-triggered reveal animations: elements fade/slide in as they enter viewport.
- Easing: cubic-bezier(0.2, 0.0, 0.2, 1) — smooth, confident, no bounce or elastic.
- Durations: 0.3-0.6s for reveals, 0.15-0.25s for hover/interaction states.
- Stagger child elements by 50-80ms for sequential reveals.
- Hero section uses a Three.js gyroscope (concentric rings on different axes, 500 particles).
- NEVER use bounce or elastic easing. NEVER use attention-seeking animations.
- Respect prefers-reduced-motion.

## Visual Language

- The design feel is: warm, editorial, spacious, quietly powerful.
- The logo's organic/hand-drawn quality gives permission for warmth — lean into it. Not sterile.
- Reference: Stripe's product pages, Linear's homepage, Apple's typography, McKinsey's editorial layouts.
- Avoid: dark hacker aesthetic, terminal/matrix vibes, neon accents, dense information walls.
- The hero section is the ONE place a dark background (#1A1A1A) is used. Everything else is light.
- The hand-drawn logo + geometric gyroscope is an intentional tension: organic warmth meets precision mechanics.
